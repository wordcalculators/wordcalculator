What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple number that compares your weight to your height. It is widely used as a basic indicator of whether you are underweight, within a healthy weight range, overweight, or obese. BMI does not directly measure body fat, but it correlates with more accurate methods used in research and clinical practice.

BMI categories for adults

For most adults aged 18 or older, the following cut-offs are commonly used:

Classification BMI Range (kg/m²)
Underweight < 18.5
Normal weight 18.5 – 24.9
Overweight 25.0 – 29.9
Obesity (Class I) 30.0 – 34.9
Obesity (Class II) 35.0 – 39.9
Obesity (Class III) ≥ 40.0

Limitations of BMI

BMI is a useful screening tool but not a diagnosis. It does not distinguish between muscle and fat mass, and it does not consider where fat is stored in the body. Very muscular people, athletes, pregnant people, children, and some ethnic groups may have BMI values that do not fully reflect their health status.

  • Does not account for age, ethnicity, or muscle mass.
  • May overestimate risk in very muscular individuals.
  • May underestimate risk when most fat is around the waist.

Staying within a healthy range

A healthy weight is usually supported by a balanced diet, regular physical activity, enough sleep, and stress management. Small, consistent changes are often more effective than extreme diets or sudden exercise plans.
